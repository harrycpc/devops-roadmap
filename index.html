01 Strategy

Read the market correctly

Most "DevOps Engineer" ads in Brisbane are pitched mid-to-senior, and pure-junior DevOps roles are rare. So aim at the doors that actually open for you.

door 1 · primary

Experienced-hire roles

Apply to SWE, cloud, platform, and build/release jobs at companies with real DevOps culture, then move toward it. Your 4 years count here.

door 2 · backup

Grad / early-career

Useful fallback, but they pay less and can screen out anyone who looks "too senior." Don't make this your main bet.

your edge

You can already code

The common weak spot in DevOps hires is weak engineering. You cleared that bar — lean on it in every conversation.

the goal

One real running system

A live project you built and operate outweighs a stack of tutorials. It's resume, story, and system-design practice in one.

04 The build

One end-to-end project

Take an app you can deploy — ideally one of your own frontend apps with a small backend — and run it through the whole pipeline. The last stage matters most.

1 · Containerize

Package the app with Docker so it runs the same anywhere.

2 · Provision

Write Terraform to stand up the AWS infrastructure — no clicking in the console.

3 · Pipeline

GitHub Actions builds, tests, and deploys on every push.

4 · Deploy live

Put it on a real URL with basic monitoring. Not a toy that runs once on your laptop.

5 · Operate it ◆

Maintain it. Let it break. Diagnose the failure and write up how you fixed it — that part can't be faked.

05 Proof of work

Proving it's yours in the AI era

AI raised the floor — generic code is commodity now. The signal moved up to judgment and operations, the layer AI can't fake. Don't fight it; demonstrate the part it can't do.

01

Don't prove you avoided AI

That's the wrong game. Show you use it well and still catch its mistakes: "I scaffolded the Terraform with AI, then rewrote the security groups it left wide open."

02

Make it real and operate it

A genuinely deployed, genuinely running system is hard to fake. AI writes the config — it doesn't take the pager. The outage you diagnosed is unfakeable signal.

03

The interview is the authentication

Build what you understand cold. "Why ECS not EKS?" "What changes at 100x traffic?" A real project answers follow-ups forever; a generated one collapses. Document your reasoning and dead-ends.
